Why OCBS exists

Good decisions should not depend on messy data.

Small businesses, churches, nonprofits, and service organizations often run on spreadsheets, one-off reports, and manual processes that grew over time. Those tools can work for a while, but eventually they become hard to audit, hard to explain, and hard to trust.

OCBS helps close that gap with cleanup, reporting, dashboards, automation, and local-first database or application work that fits the size of the organization.

Founder

Dalton Orahood

Dalton Orahood founded OCBS to bring practical analytics, reporting, and process improvement support to organizations that need useful systems without enterprise-level complexity.

His background in mathematics, business, data analytics, and workflow improvement shapes the way OCBS approaches projects: define the problem, simplify the data, build only what is useful, and document the system so it can be maintained after the project is finished.

OCBS is intentionally founder-led. Prospects work directly with Dalton during discovery, scope definition, build decisions, and handoff rather than being passed through layers of sales and delivery staff.

Mission and values

Clear, useful, and built for the long run.

Practical first

Transparent work

Long-term usefulness

Honest scope
